Andrej Karpathy is an AI researcher and educator whose work has shaped modern artificial intelligence. Born on October 23, 1986, in Bratislava, Czechoslovakia (now Slovakia), he has made significant contributions across academia and industry, from founding OpenAI to leading Tesla's Autopilot vision team and now pioneering AI education through Eureka Labs.

Karpathy was born in Bratislava and later moved to Canada. He earned his Bachelor's degree from the University of Toronto, followed by a Master's at the University of British Columbia. He completed his Ph.D. at Stanford University in 2016, where his adviser was Fei-Fei Li at the Stanford Vision Lab. His thesis, "Connecting Images and Natural Language," focused on convolutional and recurrent neural networks.

During his Ph.D., he designed and taught Stanford's first deep learning course, CS231n: Convolutional Neural Networks for Visual Recognition, which grew from 150 students in 2015 to 750 in 2017.

In 2015, Karpathy became a founding member and research scientist at OpenAI. During his first tenure (2015–2017), he specialized in deep learning and computer vision. After a period at Tesla and a sabbatical, he returned to OpenAI in February 2023, only to leave again in February 2024.

In June 2017, Karpathy joined Tesla as Director of AI and Autopilot Vision, reporting directly to Elon Musk. He led Tesla's computer vision team, handling in-house data labeling, neural network training, and deployment on Tesla's custom inference chips. His work advanced the Autopilot system toward full self-driving capability. He left Tesla in July 2022 after a sabbatical.

In 2024, Karpathy founded Eureka Labs, an AI education platform. His first course, LLM101n: Let's Build A Storyteller, is an undergraduate-level program that teaches students to build large language models from scratch in Python, C, and CUDA. The course covers everything from basic neural networks to a functioning web app similar to ChatGPT.
